How to Use
A quick guide to getting the most out of this accounting system.
📦 Import Shipments
1
Create a new shipment
Go to Import Shipments → New Shipment. Fill in the name, supplier, order date and arrival date. You can upload your supplier invoice to auto-fill everything.
2
Enter FX conversion
Type the USD amount on the invoice and the SGD you actually transferred. The effective exchange rate is calculated automatically — this captures bank and transfer fees.
3
Add freight, insurance and GST
Freight is split equally across all cases. GST is applied on top of product cost + freight. Leave GST at "No GST" if the shipment is not subject to GST.
4
Add products
Click + Add Product for each item. Type the product name, number of cases, and USD cost per case. Landed cost per unit is calculated live.
5
Apply to Stock when goods arrive
Save the shipment right after ordering. When goods physically arrive, expand the card and click 📦 Apply to Stock. This updates your store inventory and locks in the cost for profit tracking. This can only be done once per shipment.
💡 Upload your supplier invoice using Take Photo or Upload File — Claude will auto-fill the supplier, date and all line items.
💸 Expenses
1
Add an expense manually
Go to Expenses → Add Expense. Fill in the date, amount in SGD, description, category, and company (Bee Events, SWITS, or Both).
2
Scan a receipt
Click 📷 Take Photo to open your camera directly, or 📁 Upload File for a saved PDF or image. Claude reads the receipt and auto-fills the details for you to review.
3
Import Amazon orders in bulk
On Amazon: Account → Order History Reports → Request report → Download CSV. Then in Expenses click Import Amazon CSV and upload it. All orders come in at once under "Online Purchases".
💡 For shared expenses (e.g. rent split between both companies), select "Both (split equally)" — the system splits it 50/50 in all reports automatically.
📊 Product P&L
1
Revenue is pulled automatically
Once you confirm an invoice in the invoicing system, it appears here as revenue instantly. No manual entry needed.
2
Costs use FIFO (First In, First Out)
When you sell a product, its cost is taken from the oldest unsold batch first. So if you bought 100 units at $1.43 then 100 more at $1.61, the first 100 sales are costed at $1.43. This accurately reflects your real profit.
3
See profit per sale and per customer
Expand any product card to see every sale, its exact cost, and profit — even when selling the same product at different prices to different customers.
🧾 GST (SWITS only)
Bee Events is not GST-registered — no GST is charged on its sales. SWITS is GST-registered — 9% GST applies on all SWITS sales.
1
Output Tax = 9% of SWITS revenue
This is the GST you collect from customers and hold on behalf of IRAS.
2
Input Tax = GST paid on imports
When logging a shipment, select "9% GST" if it applies. That amount is tracked as input tax you can offset against output tax.
3
Net GST Payable = Output − Input
Go to GST Summary, select the quarter and year, and see exactly what to file with IRAS. Always verify with your accountant before submitting.
📤 Exporting
1
CSV — for Excel or your accountant
Downloads every transaction in full detail. Filter by month, year and company first. Opens cleanly in Excel or Google Sheets.
2
PDF — formal P&L report
Generates a professional P&L statement with your company details from the invoicing system settings. Select period and company, then click Download PDF — it opens in a new tab and prints automatically.
